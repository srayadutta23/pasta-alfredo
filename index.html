Italian Legacy

The Legend of Rome

1914, Via della Scrofa

Fettuccine Alfredo was created by Roman restaurateur Alfredo di Lelio in a heartfelt effort to entice his pregnant wife, Ines, to eat. He took extra-fine fettuccine, tossed it with triple butter and abundant Parmigiano-Reggiano, and served it warm.

When Hollywood stars Douglas Fairbanks and Mary Pickford dined at his restaurant during their Roman honeymoon, they fell in love with the dish, spreading its fame across the globe and gifting Alfredo a gold fork and spoon.

"The simplest ingredients, executed with precision, create the most luxurious texture."

The Required Ingredients

Quality First
Fettuccine Pasta
400 grams

High-quality bronze-die extruded or fresh egg pasta works best to bind the velvety emulsion.

Parmigiano Cheese
150 grams

Aged 24 months. Finely grated Parmigiano-Reggiano melts smoothly without clumping.

Unsalted Butter
80 grams

European-style butter with high butterfat content provides rich emulsification qualities.

Heavy Cream
200 ml

Double cream or heavy whipping cream for standard thick and rich American-style sauce texture.

Garlic Cloves
3 cloves

Finely minced. Sautéed very gently in melted butter to infuse a soft sweet flavor aroma.

Garnish & Pepper
To taste garnish

Fresh flat-leaf parsley chopped finely, combined with fresh coarse toasted black pepper.

Step 1 of 4 Preparation

Boil & Cook Fettuccine

Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Salt it generously until it tastes like the ocean. Drop your fettuccine pasta in. Stir immediately to prevent the strands from sticking together.

Boil the pasta for approximately 9 minutes, or until it reaches a perfect al dente state (tender with a firm core). Crucial tip: Reserve 1 cup of starchy pasta boiling water before draining!

Boiling Timer
09:00
Step 2 of 4 Aromatics

Melt Butter & Sauté Garlic

While the pasta boils, place a large deep skillet over medium-low heat. Add the unsalted butter and let it melt slowly without browning.

Once the butter is melted and foaming slightly, add the finely minced garlic. Sauté very gently for about 2 minutes. The garlic should soften and smell extremely fragrant, but do not let it color or burn, as this ruins the cream's clean profile.

Sauté Timer
02:00
Step 3 of 4 Sauce Base

Cream & Cheese Integration

Pour the heavy cream into the skillet containing garlic and butter. Stir well and increase the heat slightly to bring the cream to a gentle simmer.

Simmer the sauce for about 3 minutes until it reduces slightly and thickens. Turn the heat down to low. Gradually whisk in your finely grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese in small handfuls, stirring constantly until the cheese melts and emulsifies into a smooth, velvety cream sauce.

Simmer Timer
03:00
Step 4 of 4 Emulsification

Combine, Toss & Garnish

Transfer the freshly cooked, drained fettuccine directly into the skillet with the velvety sauce. Turn off the heat completely (residual heat is enough to bind the cheese emulsion).

Toss the pasta dynamically with tongs. If the sauce is too thick, splash in small amounts of the reserved starchy pasta water. The starch will emulsify with the fat, creating a silky coat over the pasta. Season with black pepper and fresh parsley. Serve immediately in warm bowls!

Chef's Secrets

The Golden Rules of Alfredo

Pro Cooking Techniques
I

Emulsification is Key

Authentic Alfredo uses pasta water to emulsify the fat of cheese and butter. Avoid boiling cheese directly on high heat, which causes it to split and turn stringy.

II

The Cheese Quality

Do not use pre-grated shaker cheese. It contains starch coating agents (cellulose) that prevent it from melting smoothly. Grate fresh Parmigiano on a microplane.

III

Warm Bowls

Alfredo cools down rapidly due to the butterfat base. Serve it in pre-warmed bowls to preserve the silky flow of the sauce from the first bite to the last.
